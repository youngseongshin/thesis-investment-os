# Investment Philosophy

Thesis OS does not hard-code one investor's style, but the default public philosophy is influenced by three schools of thought.

## Munger: Latticework Of Mental Models

Use multiple models instead of one story.

Applied as:

- Lattice judgment agent
- evidence grading
- base-rate checks
- counterarguments
- incentive and market-structure analysis

## William O'Neil: Strength, Timing, And Loss Discipline

Do not ignore price and volume. Do not average down blindly. Respect leadership, relative strength, and invalidation.

Applied as:

- screener features
- relative strength and volume expansion
- chase/extension risk
- invalidation conditions
- forward performance review

## Stanley Druckenmiller: Concentration, Flexibility, And Asymmetry

Conviction matters, but only when evidence, timing, and risk/reward align. Be willing to change when facts change.

Applied as:

- concentrated strategy review
- increase/hold/decrease/exit roundtable
- risk budget awareness
- thesis drift checks
- feedback by horizon

## Public Policy

This repository provides a framework, not financial advice. Users should adapt philosophy and rules to their own constraints.
